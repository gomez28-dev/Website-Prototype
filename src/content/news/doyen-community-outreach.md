At the core of the DOYEN Group of Companies is a belief that true growth is built on strong foundations. On June 6, 2026, we put that principle into action by hosting a gift-giving and community outreach program at **Sitero Francisco Memorial National High School**.

As a leading organization providing essential services—from premium tires and battery solutions to clean fuels, tanks, and transport equipment—we recognize that our success is directly linked to the well-being of the communities that support us. This program was designed to offer tangible support to the students, faculty, and families of the school, reinforcing our commitment to corporate social responsibility (CSR) and community-driven progress.

## Strengthening Community Bonds

The event brought together DOYEN employees, volunteers, school administrators, and local student leaders for a day of sharing, mutual support, and fun interactive activities. The outreach included the distribution of academic supplies, hygiene kits, and nourishing meals to students, ensuring they have the fundamental resources necessary to succeed in their educational pursuits.

We selected Sitero Francisco Memorial National High School because we believe that empowering local public schools is one of the most effective ways to nurture future leaders and technical specialists. Investing in education today creates the skilled professionals who will build, sustain, and drive the businesses of tomorrow.

> "True corporate growth is not measured solely by financial metrics or market share. It is measured by the positive footprint we leave in the lives of the people and communities around us. We are proud to partner with local institutions like Sitero Francisco Memorial National High School to help make a positive difference."
> — **DOYEN Management Team**

## Looking Forward

This outreach initiative is part of an ongoing commitment by the DOYEN Group of Companies to extend our impact beyond our commercial operations. Moving forward, we plan to establish regular partnerships with schools, community organizations, and environmental groups across Luzon and the wider Philippine archipelago.

We would like to extend our deepest gratitude to the teachers, volunteers, and school administrators whose coordination and warm hospitality made this day a resounding success. Through active engagement and collaboration, we look forward to driving both industrial performance and community development hand-in-hand.
